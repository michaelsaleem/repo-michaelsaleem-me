About Me
My Story

Before working in Information Technology, I worked in consumer retail for 8 years. It was here where I learned the importance of customer service. I used to sell digital SLR cameras, and I remember having to provide product demonstrations while explaining all the technical jargon (aperture, shutter speed, ISO etc) in layman's terms.

In 2013, I then started my first job in the IT industry working in a Data Centre as an Operations Technician (DCOPS). I think it's fair to say that the amount of technical jargon that I was exposed to exponentially increased compared to when I was working in the retail sector; but, I absolutely loved it! Building and racking servers, cabling and performing a basic level of troubleshooting was actually really enjoyable

Still working in the same company at that point, I then progressed into different roles such as 2nd Line Support Analyst and then moved into a Network Security Engineer / Architect role. I predominantly specialise in load balancers such as the F5 BIG-IP and firewalls such as Palo Alto NGFW and Cisco Firepower.

That brings me to where I am today (with a wife and a couple of kids thrown into the mix for good measure üòÜ). Nowadays, I try to continously learn technologies every day - such as cloud, cyber security and automation.

I am a huge believer in knowledge sharing; I do my best to impart any knowledge that I have with others. I am a regular contributor to the F5 DevCentral community, where I am currently an MVP.

PCNSE
Palo Alto Networks

The PCNSE certification validates the knowledge and skills required for network security engineers who design, deploy, operate, manage, and troubleshoot Palo Alto Networks firewalls. PCNSE certified individuals have demonstrated in-depth knowledge of the Palo Alto Networks product portfolio and can make full use of it in the vast majority of implementations. The PCNSE validates that engineers can correctly deploy firewalls while leveraging the rest of the platform.

PCNSA
Palo Alto Networks

The PCNSA certification validates the knowledge and skills required for network security administrators responsible for deploying and operating Palo Alto Networks firewalls. PCNSA certified individuals have demonstrated knowledge of the Palo Alto Networks NGFW feature set and in the Palo Alto Networks product portfolio core components. The PCNSA seeks to identify people who can operate Palo Alto Networks firewalls to protect networks from cutting-edge cyber threats.
